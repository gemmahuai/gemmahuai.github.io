YO-YO SITE

Yo-yoing has been a fun part of my childhood. I was inspired by a Chinese cartoon series 火力少年王 (translated as "Blazing Teens"). My passion for yo-yos lasted through the end of middle school. Those years were filled with excitement as my friends and I traded tricks, engaged in rivalries, and even skipped classes to polish our skills. The thrill of learning new tricks and the camaraderie we shared made those moments memorable, even though my yo-yo was confiscated on several occasions by primary school teachers...



The modern world of yo-yo includes a range of styles, each with its unique flair: 1A (unresponsive), 2A (looping), 3A (two-handed string tricks), 4A (off-string), and 5A (freehand). While I primarily focus on 1A and 5A, I’m still far from reaching world-class proficiency. Below, I share some of my personal insights and experiences with yo-yoing that I find particularly enjoyable.
